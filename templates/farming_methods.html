Traditional Paddy Cultivation
For: Rice

Conventional method of growing rice in flooded fields with manual transplanting of seedlings.

Steps:
  1. Prepare nursery beds and sow seeds
  2. Prepare main field with plowing and puddling
  3. Transplant 20-25 day old seedlings
  4. Maintain water level of 2-5 cm
  5. Apply fertilizers as per recommendation
  6. Harvest when 80% of grains turn golden yellow
Benefits:
  • Well-established method with predictable results
  • Effective weed control through flooding
  • Suitable for areas with abundant water
Resources Needed:
  • Abundant water supply
  • Chemical fertilizers (NPK)
  • Pesticides for pest control
  • Labor for transplanting and harvesting
Furrow Irrigation
For: Cotton, Sugarcane

Traditional method of irrigating row crops by creating channels between rows.

Steps:
  1. Prepare land with proper slope
  2. Create furrows between crop rows
  3. Release water at the higher end of the field
  4. Allow water to flow through furrows
  5. Close the inlet when water reaches the end of furrows
Benefits:
  • Simple and low-cost method
  • No specialized equipment needed
  • Suitable for row crops
Resources Needed:
  • Water source
  • Labor for creating and maintaining furrows
  • Proper field grading equipment
System of Rice Intensification (SRI)
For: Rice

Modern method that uses less water, seeds, and chemical inputs while increasing yield.

Steps:
  1. Transplant young seedlings (8-12 days old)
  2. Plant single seedlings with wider spacing (25x25 cm)
  3. Apply alternate wetting and drying instead of continuous flooding
  4. Use organic manures and compost
  5. Perform mechanical weeding to aerate soil
Benefits:
  • 30-50% water savings
  • 85-90% seed reduction
  • Increased yield by 20-50%
  • Better root growth and stronger plants
  • Reduced methane emissions
Resources Needed:
  • Rotary weeder
  • Organic compost
  • Marker for precise spacing
Drip Irrigation
For: Vegetables, Fruits, Cotton

Modern water-efficient irrigation method that delivers water directly to plant roots.

Steps:
  1. Install main line, sub-main lines, and laterals
  2. Place drippers near plant roots
  3. Install filters to prevent clogging
  4. Set up controller for automated irrigation
  5. Regularly flush the system to remove sediments
Benefits:
  • Water savings of 30-70%
  • Reduced weed growth
  • Improved crop quality and yield
  • Ability to apply fertilizers through the system (fertigation)
  • Reduced labor costs
Resources Needed:
  • Drip irrigation kit
  • Water source and pump
  • Filters and pressure regulators
  • Timer or controller (optional)

Choosing the Right Farming Method

Selecting the appropriate farming method depends on various factors including your local climate, soil type, water availability, and market demand. Here are some considerations to help you decide:

Conventional Methods
  • Advantages: Well-established, predictable results, often higher immediate yields
  • Disadvantages: Higher water and chemical input requirements, potential environmental impact
  • Best for: Farmers with abundant water, access to inputs, and established markets
Modern/Organic Methods
  • Advantages: Resource efficiency, environmental sustainability, premium market prices
  • Disadvantages: May require new skills, initial investment, and transition period
  • Best for: Areas with water scarcity, environmentally sensitive regions, premium markets
Consider starting with a small plot to test new methods before implementing them across your entire farm.
