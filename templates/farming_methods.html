Traditional Paddy Cultivation Conventional
For: Rice

Conventional method of growing rice in flooded fields with manual transplanting of seedlings.

Steps:
  1. Prepare nursery beds and sow seeds
  2. Prepare main field with plowing and puddling
  3. Transplant 20-25 day old seedlings
  4. Maintain water level of 2-5 cm
  5. Apply fertilizers as per recommendation
  6. Harvest when 80% of grains turn golden yellow
Benefits:
  • Well-established method with predictable results
  • Effective weed control through flooding
  • Suitable for areas with abundant water
System of Rice Intensification (SRI) Modern
For: Rice

Modern method that uses less water, seeds, and chemical inputs while increasing yield.

Steps:
  1. Transplant young seedlings (8-12 days old)
  2. Plant single seedlings with wider spacing (25x25 cm)
  3. Apply alternate wetting and drying
  4. Use organic manures and compost
  5. Perform mechanical weeding to aerate soil
Benefits:
  • 30-50% water savings
  • 85-90% seed reduction
  • Increased yield by 20-50%
  • Reduced methane emissions

Choosing the Right Method

Conventional

Best for areas with abundant water, access to chemical inputs, and established markets. Predictable yields but higher resource requirements.

Modern/Organic

Ideal for water-scarce regions, sustainable farming goals, and premium organic markets. Lower input costs but requires more knowledge and transition time.
